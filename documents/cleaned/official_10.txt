## On-campus advantages
Living on campus makes it easier to meet people through roommates, floor events, and First Year Residential Experience programming. Residence halls are monitored around the clock with Campus Safety at entrances, which can feel reassuring in New York City.

## Convenience of dorm life
On-campus housing keeps students close to classes and dining. Some students value the shorter commute and built-in dining hall access during cold weather.

## Off-campus advantages
Living off campus can support independence: cooking, decorating, and learning the city through apartment hunting. NYU provides apartment search resources to help students evaluate listings.

## Cost and decision factors
Cost is a major factor for both options. On-campus housing is expensive, but off-campus costs vary widely by neighborhood, apartment size, roommates, utilities, and broker fees. Students should research budget, commute tolerance, roommate plans, and readiness for lease logistics before deciding.

## Advice for choosing
Students should list needs such as distance to campus, roommate situation, and budget, then compare realistic options rather than assuming one choice is always cheaper or better.
